Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and founder of the Grameen Bank. Known as the "Father of Microfinance," he developed a revolutionary system of lending small amounts of money to impoverished individuals without collateral, enabling them to start or expand small businesses. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus earned his Ph.D. in Economics from Vanderbilt University. He returned to Bangladesh in the 1970s and initiated experiments with microcredit, which evolved into the Grameen Bank in 1983.In 2006, Dr. Yunus and the Grameen Bank were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to combat poverty through inclusive economic opportunities.
